Joey Logano: 2-time NASCAR Cup Series Champion

TL;DR: Joey Logano is an American professional stock car racing driver, known for winning two NASCAR Cup Series championships in 2018 and 2022. He achieved 31 career Cup Series victories by 2024, as reported by NASCAR.

Joey Logano is an American professional stock car racing driver, competing full-time in the NASCAR Cup Series. He has secured two NASCAR Cup Series championships, in 2018 and 2022, and 31 career Cup Series wins by 2024, according to NASCAR.

Logano is a prominent figure in American professional stock car racing. He competes full-time in the NASCAR Cup Series, driving the No. 22 Ford Mustang for Team Penske. Logano's career highlights include multiple championships and significant race victories.

His career began at a young age, quickly progressing through various racing divisions. Logano earned the nickname "Sliced Bread" early in his career, indicating his perceived potential as "the best thing since sliced bread" within the sport. What are Joey Logano's major career milestones?

Logano has achieved significant milestones including two NASCAR Cup Series championships, 31 Cup Series race wins, and a Daytona 500 victory in 2015, making him one of 41 drivers to win the event, as documented by Daytona International Speedway.

His success also extends to the NASCAR Xfinity Series, where he holds 30 career wins, as documented by Racing-Reference.

Joey Logano won the 2015 Daytona 500, a prestigious event that only 41 different drivers have won since its inception in 1959, according to Daytona International Speedway.

Which teams has Joey Logano competed for?

Joey Logano has primarily competed for two major teams in the NASCAR Cup Series: Joe Gibbs Racing from 2008 to 2012, and Team Penske from 2013 to the present, accumulating 29 of his 31 Cup Series victories with Team Penske, according to Racing-Reference.

Joey Logano began his full-time NASCAR Cup Series career with Joe Gibbs Racing in 2009. He drove the No. 20 car for the team, securing two Cup Series victories during his tenure from 2008 to 2012, as reported by Racing-Reference. Logano transitioned to Team Penske in 2013, taking over the No. 22 Ford. This move marked a significant turning point in his career, leading to his two Cup Series championships. He has achieved 29 of his 31 career Cup Series wins with Team Penske, establishing a long-standing and successful partnership with the organization, according to Team Penske.
